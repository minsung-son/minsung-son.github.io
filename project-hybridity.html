Embracing Hybridity: A Gallery for Things that Don't Fit

Architecture / 2023.07

Project Description

Embracing Hybridity proposes a gallery extension to the Grade 1 listed Fitzwilliam Museum to house artworks and objects that do not fit into conventional curatorial categories. I take issue with simplistic temporal or geographical categorisations of museum objects, since this museological practice systematically excludes hybrid objects, produced by more than one culture or author, from the exhibited spaces of museums. The new gallery space seeks to offer a celebratory space for such objects, while also providing a new accessible entrance to the museum. The elevated main structure, clearly distinct from the existing building, wraps around the walls of the underused central courtyard and reorientates the plan around this space—which acquires a new role as a vibrant space leading to the new central entrance—instead of around the authoritarian portico entrance. The facade, made up of several layers formed by metal mesh, translucent glass, and clear glazing, allows those looking from outside to clearly see both the new gallery’s celebration of hybrid objects and the historic walls of the existing building. Simultaneously, the superimposition of the layers creates suggestive and interesting viewports through which what happens inside the gallery is only partially seen.
