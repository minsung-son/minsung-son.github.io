Reimagining Seoul Morphology Through Scale and Openness

Architecture / 2023.07

Project Description

The strength of generative AI lies in its ability to generate countless generations based on training data. In this project, this strength is exploited to imagine alternative urban morphologies for Seoul. Using StyleGAN and 3DGAN algorithms trained with the data from Seoul’s existing city blocks, new blocks that reflect the morphological characteristics of Seoul as a city are generated.

These generated blocks are then classified according to their urban scale and degree of openness, the two main variables that determine the morphology of a city block. With the vision of a diversified Seoul where different scales and levels of openness co-exist, the blocks were in turn patched together on a 4x4 grid to complete the hypothetical picture of the city. In doing so, a unique ruleset—inspired by the principles of cellular automata—ensuring diversities of scale and openness, both city-wide and within smaller neighbourhoods, was implemented to allow an autonomous evolution of the city towards a diversified arrangement of blocks. In order to imagine what such a diversified city would look like for those living in it, StableDiffusion is used to generate realistic renderings from the abstract blocks patched on the grid.
