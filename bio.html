Minsung Son (b. 2001, Seoul) is an Architecture B.A. (Hons) graduate at the University of Cambridge, currently at Kengo Kuma & Associates Tokyo. He is also an independent photographer, videographer, and writer. He has graduated with first class honours.

Minsung's architectural interests span across practice and theory. As a designer, he is experienced in research-driven concept design, with a particular focus on cultural and educational institutions and the innate public potential they bear. In architectural theory, Minsung is interested in the symbolic and practical roles of modernist architecture in 20th century's newly independent nations, and in the global variations of and additions to modernist architecture as responses to local socioeconomic contexts. His undergraduate dissertation, entitled Nation Building in the Balcony: A Postcolonial Critique of Resident-led Extensions to South Korean Modern Housing, has unanimously won the David Roberts Memorial Prize for best dissertation at the University of Cambridge Department of Architecture, and is due to be published as a journal article.

Currently based in Seoul, Korea, and Tokyo, Japan.
