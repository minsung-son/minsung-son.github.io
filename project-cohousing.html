Co-Housing as an Alternative Care Community

Architecture / 2023.03

Project Description

Co-housing as an Alternative Care Community is a dynamic scheme in Mill Road, Cambridge, where those not supported by traditional family structures live together to give and receive care at personal, communal, and wider social levels. I was inspired by Leslie Kern’s book Feminist City, which points out that the work of caring for others has become ‘assigned’ to women as a result of the traditional family structure. This both places great care pressure on women, and limits care to within the family when it can be much more far-reaching. I sought to create a space where care work becomes mutual and diverse, happening organically without reliance to existing family structures and gender roles. The scheme empowers residents to delve into their personal passions and hobbies, while also connecting meaningfully with other residents and with the local neighbourhood. In doing so, they will learn to distribute, give, and receive care mutually, experiencing a more equitable and fulfilling lifestyle.
