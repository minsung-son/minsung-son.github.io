Unlocking the Island

Architecture / 2024.06

Project Description

The proposal is Fitzwilliam Museum’s satellite location in Chesterton to bring art closer to local life. Unlike its grand city-centre presence, this new museum will integrate seamlessly with the community. The Victoria Road Island, a derelict building in the heart of Chesterton, is a prime location for this mission. The site’s size and lack of programme divide adjacent blocks and limit walkability, calling for a casually visited museum. Revitalising this space involves creating paths through the previously monolithic area, improving urban connectivity. Historic maps guide the reintroduction of paths, blending the site’s past with its present to inform new forms and spaces. What was once very monolithic and closed is exploded to form an intensely open, smaller-scale network of spaces. Most of the site is now completely open public pathway. The transformed ground floor will feature open storage for artworks, an art unloading area, project spaces, a workshop, a café, a bookshop, and a museum store. Visitors can explore freely, discovering artworks through strategically placed sightlines that encourage interaction. The design includes partial roof removal and a new first-floor gallery, creating a mix of open and enclosed spaces varied in heights and atmospheres. The long-term proposal involves pedestrianising Chesterton Road, enhancing museum access and connecting with shops across the road through public art and community events.
